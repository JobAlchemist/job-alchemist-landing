WHAT YOU GET AS AN EMPLOYER

Support your people with care and reduce risks.

  • Reduce organisational risk - and actively support those who remain. A structured, visible support offer reduces the risk of ER complaints and reputational damage. And when people see colleagues treated with dignity through a redundancy, it strengthens - rather than undermines - the psychological safety of the whole team.
  • Give managers something real to offer, not just a conversation to survive. Most line managers aren't equipped to answer "so what do I do now?" after a notification conversation. JobAlchemist gives them a clear, credible answer - without requiring them to become job-search advisers or follow up individually.
  • Something employees can act on immediately - not in three weeks. Speed matters for the employee's confidence and momentum. It also matters for yours. The faster someone has something practical to act on, the less likely they are to stay in a place of uncertainty.
  • A consistent process across your whole cohort. Whether you're letting go of three people or thirty, every impacted employee gets the same structured starting point. That consistency matters legally, operationally, and reputationally. No one slips through because a manager forgot to follow up.
  • Complements your internal capability - doesn't replace it. JobAlchemist handles the practical job-search support so your HR team can focus on what only they can do: the conversations, the legal process, the internal transitions. It's an addition to what you're already doing, not a hand-off.

WHY WE BUILT THIS

JobAlchemist started when Suhel's wife was made redundant. She was spending four to five hours a day on job search - finding roles, assessing fit, trying to tailor applications in ChatGPT, tracking everything in spreadsheets. She was exhausted. That weekend, we built a prototype to take some of that weight off.

A few friends asked for access. Then a few more. When we started the beta, users told us unprompted that it would be valuable for employers supporting staff through redundancy - because they'd been through it themselves, or watched colleagues go through it. That shaped what JobAlchemist is now: something built to help both the person going through the transition, and the employer who wants to do right by them.

We've been through redundancies. We've watched friends go through them. We built this because the need is real and the existing options are too slow and too expensive for most situations.

That's why the employer offering matters as much as the product itself. When an organisation is going through a restructure, the support it provides says something real about how it treats people - and we wanted to make that support something employees would actually open and use.
