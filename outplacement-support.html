The moment HR leaders dread - and what usually gets offered.

When a restructure happens, HR teams move fast. Notifications go out. Meetings are held. Paperwork is signed. And then the impacted employee is on their own - with whatever support the company could pull together in time.

Traditional outplacement is the standard answer. But it's expensive, often underused, and built around a coaching model that takes weeks to get going. By the time someone has their first session, they've already spent two weeks in a fog.

The gap isn't in intention. It's in timing. People need something they can open that afternoon.

"The hiring manager has moved on. Your employee hasn't. That window closes faster than most people realise."
WHAT YOUR PEOPLE GET

Something useful. From day one.

Employees access JobAlchemist directly and privately. There's no waiting list, no intake form, no coach assigned. They upload their CV, paste in a job ad, and get structured, role-specific guidance within minutes.

HOW THIS IS DIFFERENT

Traditional outplacement is built around coaching. This is built around doing.

A coaching programme is valuable. But it starts in week two or three, after intake, matching, and scheduling. The employee's momentum - and their confidence - has already dipped.

JobAlchemist is different because it's immediate and task-focused. Upload a CV. Paste a job ad. Get structured guidance on fit, tailoring, and how to present your experience. No intake. No waiting room.

JobAlchemist started because my wife was made redundant. She was spending four to five hours a day on job search - finding roles, assessing fit, trying to tailor applications in ChatGPT, tracking everything in spreadsheets. She was exhausted. So I built a prototype in a weekend.

A few friends asked for access. Then a few more. When we started the beta, users told us unprompted that it would be valuable for employers supporting staff through redundancy - because they'd been through it themselves, or watched colleagues go through it. That shaped what JobAlchemist is now: something built to help both the person going through the transition, and the employer who wants to do right by them.
